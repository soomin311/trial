Digital Animation

Physics concepts brought to life through motion, timing, and visual storytelling

Gravity & Air Resistance Animation

Project Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y71WSPkRVI4

Project Overview

For this digital animation project, I visualized a physics concept by comparing the falling motion of a plastic ball and a steel ball. The animation is designed to challenge common intuition through a simple but thought-provoking question: “Which one falls faster?”

Design & Production Process

  • Topic Selection & Scriptwriting: Began with the idea of contrasting gravity and air resistance. The script intentionally introduces a twist—highlighting how people often assume the heavier object falls faster for the wrong reasons.
  • Adobe Animate Artwork: Created vector graphics for the background, ground, plastic ball, and steel ball. Converted all elements into symbols to prepare for animation.
  • Motion Tweens & Physics Timing: Used motion tweens to animate both balls falling at different speeds. Styled timing so the steel ball reaches the ground first due to less influence from air resistance.
  • Transitions & Visual Polish: Applied fade-outs from the intro text into the main animation. Added easing and alpha transitions for smoother motion across layers.
  • Voice-Over Synchronization: Recorded narration and imported it into Animate. Aligned the audio precisely on the timeline using the Stream sync setting.
  • Summary Screen: Designed a final screen explaining that gravity acts equally on both objects, but air resistance slows down the lighter plastic ball.
  • Export & Publishing: Exported the completed animation as an MP4 and uploaded it to YouTube.

Reflection

This project helped me explore how animation can simplify abstract scientific concepts. Working in Adobe Animate strengthened my understanding of timing, easing, and the relationship between visuals and narration. I also learned how to synchronize audio with motion and how small design decisions—like fade-ins or motion curves—can dramatically improve clarity and engagement.
